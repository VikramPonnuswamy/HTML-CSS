An introductory guide to computational thinking

In today’s modern world, computers are commonly found and utilised to its maximum capacity to enhance the experience of a human interaction. Computers are widely used to solve complex problems as such that it has become a daily toolset for everyone. Computers are electronic devices that store, retrieve and process data that has been programmed by a human using sets of mathematical abstraction and algorithms.

Computational thinking allows the human to breakdown a complex problem into smaller manageable fragments and develop possible solutions that can be understood by computers, human or both [1]. In layman’s terms, computational thinking enables a human to approach any problem in a structured manner and to develop solution(s) that are simple enough to be executed by a computer or another person.

There are four essential pillars of computational thinking:

  • Decomposition – Process of breaking down a larger complex problem into manageable bite size chunks.
  • Abstraction – Focusing on the most important information and elements of the problem. Ignoring the irrelevant and unnecessary details [2].
  • Pattern Recognition – Identifying similarities and patterns within a problem to optimise a solution. This can be done pre or post algorithm creation.
  • Algorithms – Developing a step-by-step solution to the complex problem using series of instructions that a computer can interpret and process data to solve a problem.

Why is computational thinking important to the programme of study?

Computational thinking introduces concepts such as problem solving, critical thinking, logic processing and algorithms which are all steppingstone to sound programming. In this course, there will be challenges, programming tasks, designing and implementation of solution(s) to a complex problem. Thus, understanding computational thinking tunes the mind to approach all problems in a logical structure manner. It also nurtures an individual in the world of computation and visualise it from the perspective of a computer to solve any given tasks. During this programme of the study, we are expected to study various programming languages in depth (python, html, JavaScript, etc) and computational thinking is a steppingstone to solving complex challenges in these languages.
